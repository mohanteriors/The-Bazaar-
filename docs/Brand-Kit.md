The Bazaar — Official Brand Identity & Guidelines
1. Brand Essence
Brand Name:

The Bazaar

Tagline Options:

Your Marketplace. Your Rules.

Where Vendors Grow. Where Shoppers Win.

Africa’s Most Flexible Multi-Vendor Marketplace.

Brand Positioning Statement:

The Bazaar is a modern, scalable, multi-vendor marketplace designed to empower shoppers and vendors with speed, trust, and a premium shopping experience. It blends enterprise-grade reliability with a vibrant marketplace culture that feels distinctly Kenyan yet globally competitive.

Brand Personality:

Confident

Efficient

Transparent

Growth-driven

Tech-forward

Brand Voice:

Clear, direct, professional, supportive.
Built for a platform that manages money, businesses, and consumer trust.

2. Logo Specifications
Primary Logo:

Name-only typographic logo: THE BAZAAR

Typeface: Montserrat ExtraBold (or similar geometric sans-serif)

Spacing: Wide kerning for a stable, enterprise look

Secondary Logo (Icon):

Abstract geometric “B” with two interlocking blocks

Symbolizes marketplace connections (vendors ↔ shoppers)

Usage Rules:

Minimum clear space: height of “B” on all sides

Avoid placing the logo on busy backgrounds

Do not stretch, warp, recolor, or apply effects

3. Color Palette
Primary Colors
Purpose	Color Code	Usage
Primary Brand Blue	#0057D9	Buttons, CTAs, headers
Dark Navy	#0A1A2F	Text, navigation, admin dashboards
Neutral Gray	#F2F4F7	Backgrounds, cards
Secondary Colors
Purpose	Color Code	Usage
Success Green	#2EB67D	Status, success notifications
Warning Yellow	#F4C51A	Alerts, pending approvals
Danger Red	#D92E2E	Errors, critical warnings

Contrast Rules:
Text must maintain WCAG-compliant contrast against backgrounds.
Dark Navy (#0A1A2F) is the default text color for readability.

4. Typography
Primary Typeface (UI & Brand):

Inter

Clean, modern, ideal for dashboards and marketplace interfaces

Available in all major systems and frameworks (Next.js, Tailwind)

Weights & Usage:

ExtraBold → Headings (H1, H2)

SemiBold → Section titles, callouts

Medium → Buttons, labels

Regular → Body text

Secondary Typeface (Brand & Marketing):

Montserrat

Used sparingly for marketing assets, hero banners and branding templates

5. Iconography
Style:

Rounded corners (consistent with shadcn UI)

Line thickness: 2px

Neutral color: Dark Navy / White

Use Lucide Icons as the system standard

Do's:

Keep icons simple and universally recognizable

Maintain consistent stroke width

Use color sparingly for meaning (success, error, warning)

Don'ts:

No gradients

No hyper-realistic or shaded icons

Avoid mixing icon sets

6. UI Components Design Language
Buttons

Primary: Blue (#0057D9)

Secondary: White with navy border

Danger: Red (#D92E2E)

Rounded corners: rounded-xl

Shadow: soft, subtle, non-intrusive

Forms

Rounded corners

Light gray backgrounds

Clear input borders

Always show inline validation messages

Cards

White background

Soft shadow, 8px blur

Rounded-xl

Used for product listings, dashboards, vendor metrics

Spacing System

Base unit: 8px grid system

Ensures consistent vertical and horizontal rhythm

7. Tone & Copy Guidelines
Tone Pillars:

Direct

Supportive

Trust-oriented

Informative

Examples:
Good Copy (Correct Tone):

“Your payout is being processed and will arrive by Friday.”
“Your store is performing well this week—great job!”
“Please update your business documents to complete verification.”

Bad Copy (Wrong Tone):

“You didn’t follow the rules.”
“Oops… something happened!”
“Please try again later.”

Always offer clarity and context.

8. Image Guidelines
Photography Style:

Real people

African markets, lifestyle and modern retail

Clean backgrounds

Authentic emotion

Product Images:

High resolution only

Transparent or white background

No watermark

No borders or shadows

9. Branding for Each Portal
Main App (Marketplace)

Friendly marketplace energy

Brighter tones (primary blue + accent colors)

Engaging visuals and product-first layout

Vendor Portal

Business-centric

More structured, conservative palette

Dashboard-first approach

Emphasis on clarity, profitability, and operations

Admin Portal

Serious, enterprise-grade

Dark navy UI theme

Emphasis on data, security, and monitoring

Minimalistic interface

10. Brand Do's and Don'ts
Do:

Keep everything clean and consistent

Respect spacing rules

Use the defined color system

Maintain clarity in text

Design for readability, accessibility, and speed

Don’t:

Introduce new colors unless approved

Replace typefaces

Overuse gradients or complex visuals

Use informal or playful tone

Use compressed images in the UI

12. Purpose of this Brand Kit

This brand kit serves as the single source of truth for UI, UX, marketing, and design decisions across all apps within the The Bazaar ecosystem. It ensures every portal shares the same DNA, creating trust and visual cohesion for users, vendors, and internal teams.
